Thomas J.Watson Sr. Biography

Thomas was an American industrialist who built the International Business Machines Corporation (IBM) into the largest manufacturer of electric typewriters and data-processing equipment in the world.
Watson studied at the Elmira (New York) School of Commerce and then worked as a salesman. In 1914 Watson became president of the Computing-Tabulating-Recording Company, a maker of electrical punch-card computing systems.
Watson was active in civic affairs and was noted for his efforts on behalf of the arts and world peace. In his honour, his wife started the Thomas J. Watson Foundation in 1961.
